ICCV 2019 Workshop Face Recognition in the Wild

Call for papers

       Though almost saturated performance has been achieved on several classic face recognition tasks in academia, including LFW and Megaface, there are still many open problems for        face recognition in industrial applications. For example, the training data might be quite noisy and imbalanced. Our workshop is mainly to discuss how to solve these problems. We call        for papers in the following pecific subtopics and also welcome papers for general face recognition. The submission link is here.
  • a. Large-scale face recognition
  • b. Face recognition with imbalanced training data in the low-shot learning scenario
  • c. Generative model for face synthesis
  • d. Face representation learning
  • e. Disentangled representation learning
  • f. Training with noise labels
  • g. Image understanding with knowledge base
  • h. 3D analysis & synthesis
  • i. Expression analysis
  • j. How humans and face verification algorithms can work together
  • k. Bias in face recognition
